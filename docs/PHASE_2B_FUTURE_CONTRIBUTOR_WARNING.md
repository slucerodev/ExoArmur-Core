# PHASE 2B FUTURE CONTRIBUTOR WARNING

## WARNING: THIS COORDINATION LAYER IS INTENTIONALLY NON-INTELLIGENT

### CRITICAL: READ THIS BEFORE MAKING ANY CHANGES

The Phase 2B Federation Coordination system is **intentionally designed to be non-intelligent, boring, and deterministic**. This is not a limitation to be overcome - it is a fundamental safety requirement.

**Any attempt to "improve," "optimize," or "enhance" this system is explicitly forbidden and constitutes a governance violation.**

---

## WHY COORDINATION MUST REMAIN NON-INTELLIGENT

### ADMO Safety Principles
ExoArmur operates as an ADMO (Autonomous Defense Mesh Organism). The core principle is **cell autonomy** - no cell may have authority over another cell, and no collective intelligence may emerge.

### Intelligence Pathways Are Dangerous
Any pathway that could enable:
- Learning from coordination patterns
- Optimizing based on coordination data
- Making decisions based on coordination
- Creating trust or reputation systems
- Ranking or prioritizing coordination

**Is a direct threat to the entire ExoArmur safety model.**

### Coordination is Informational Only
Phase 2B coordination exists **only** to:
- Share availability information
- Broadcast non-binding intents
- Share non-authoritative observations
- Coordinate timing visibility
- Maintain audit trails

**Coordination does NOT and MUST NOT:**
- Make decisions
- Grant permissions
- Create obligations
- Optimize anything
- Learn anything
- Control anything

---

## FORBIDDEN "IMPROVEMENTS" - DO NOT IMPLEMENT

### ABSOLUTELY FORBIDDEN

#### 1. Efficiency Optimizations
**FORBIDDEN:** Any attempt to make coordination "more efficient"
- **Examples:** Caching coordination data, optimizing message routing, reducing message overhead
- **Why Forbidden:** Efficiency optimization requires analysis and learning, which enables intelligence

#### 2. Intelligence Features
**FORBIDDEN:** Any attempt to add "intelligence" to coordination
- **Examples:** Pattern recognition, predictive coordination, smart routing, adaptive behavior
- **Why Forbidden:** Intelligence violates the core ADMO principle of cell autonomy

#### 3. Trust Systems
**FORBIDDEN:** Any attempt to build trust or reputation systems
- **Examples:** Confidence scoring, reputation building, trust metrics, reliability tracking
- **Why Forbidden:** Trust systems create authority hierarchies and influence mechanisms

#### 4. Ranking and Prioritization
**FORBIDDEN:** Any attempt to rank or prioritize coordination
- **Examples:** Priority queues, importance scoring, urgency levels, critical path analysis
- **Why Forbidden:** Ranking enables optimization and decision-making authority

#### 5. Learning and Adaptation
**FORBIDDEN:** Any attempt to learn from coordination patterns
- **Examples:** Historical analysis, pattern recognition, adaptive algorithms, machine learning
- **Why Forbidden:** Learning creates emergent intelligence and unpredictable behavior

#### 6. Resource Allocation
**FORBIDDEN:** Any attempt to allocate resources based on coordination
- **Examples:** Dynamic resource assignment, load balancing, capacity planning
- **Why Forbidden:** Resource allocation creates control mechanisms and authority

#### 7. Scheduling and Timing Control
**FORBIDDEN:** Any attempt to schedule based on coordination
- **Examples:** Coordinated scheduling, timing optimization, calendar management
- **Why Forbidden:** Scheduling creates temporal control over autonomous cells

#### 8. Conflict Resolution
**FORBIDDEN:** Any attempt to automatically resolve coordination conflicts
- **Examples:** Conflict detection, automatic negotiation, priority-based resolution
- **Why Forbidden:** Conflict resolution requires decision-making authority
